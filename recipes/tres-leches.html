Tres Leches

Description

A tres leches cake is a beloved Latin American dessert known for its indulgent moistness and rich flavor. It consists of a light, airy sponge cake that is soaked in a combination of three milks: evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and whole milk. This soaking process infuses the cake with a sweet, creamy, and slightly caramel-like flavor, resulting in a dessert that is both decadent and satisfying. Topped with whipped cream or fruit, this cake is a delightful treat that beautifully balances sweetness and richness, making it a favorite for special occasions and gatherings.


Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 11/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 11/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 12-ounce can of evaporated milk
  • 14-ounce can of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup whole milk

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a mixing bowl, beat 4 large eggs and 1 cup of granulated sugar together until the mixture is light and fluffy.
  3. Stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour and 1 1/2 teaspoons of baking powder.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the egg and sugar mixture, mixing until just combined.
  6. Pour the cake batter into the prepared baking dish and spread it evenly.
  7. Bake the cake in the preheated oven for about 30-35 minutes, or until it is golden and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cake to cool slightly, then use a fork to poke holes all over the surface.
  9. In a separate bowl, mix together a 12-ounce can of evaporated milk, a 14-ounce can of sweetened condensed milk, and 1 cup of whole milk.
  10. Pour the three-milk mixture over the cake, ensuring it soaks in.
  11. Refrigerate the cake for at least 4 hours, or overnight for best results to allow it to absorb the milk mixture.
  12. When serving, you can top the cake with whipped cream if desired.
  13. Slice, serve, and enjoy your homemade tres leches cake!
